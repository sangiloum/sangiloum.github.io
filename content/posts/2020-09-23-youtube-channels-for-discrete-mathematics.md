Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, many seminars are now held online. Many of them made a YouTube channel to post their videos. But it is very difficult to search for such videos. In this post, I’d like to share a few of them. Please suggest more in the comment.

  - [IBS Discrete Mathematics Group](https://youtube.com/c/ibsdimag)
  - [Department of Combinatorics and Optimization at University of Waterloo](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCYbrNs9lMqGw65TlzTRJuEw)
  - [Oxford Discrete Mathematics and Probability Seminar, Oxford University](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCxCbrvWXOxV0tXf2BDMWFyQ)
  - [Laboratory of Combinatorial and Geometric Structures, MIPT](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCvYDu4CVM_RdBCEFGk17cMQ)
  - [Discrete Math Seminar at the University of South Carolina](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCEgVKbuYvr8Ic7BsENiH3Bg)
  - [Discrete Math Seminar at the Simon Fraser University](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCfFEKEZsDvuYz1HA3-WCptg)
  - [ACO Program, Georgia Tech](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCXpZUSJIcBqvVclvQX90X7g)
  - [SCMS Combinatorics Seminar, Shanghai Center for Mathematical Sciences, Fudan University](https://space.bilibili.com/556006423/channel/detail?cid=133341) (on Bilibili.com)
  - [PIMS-UVic Discrete Math Seminar](https://mathtube.org/conference/PIMS-UVic%20Discrete%20Math%20Seminar) (on [mathtube.org](https://mathtube.org/))
  - Graphs and Optimization seminar, LaBRI (on [its own server](https://webconf.u-bordeaux.fr/b/mar-ef4-zed))
